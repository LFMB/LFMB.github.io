State of the Union

This is the beginning to figuring out why some communities - in particular states - have noticeable variances in general wealth and opportunity. The catalyst to this research is Governor LePage’s comment made first in 2010 and subsequently harped on for at least four years about electrical costs being the key factor in holding Maine’s economy back.

Before you can start looking for causes to a situation you first have to eliminate the possibility that the outcome can be explained by naturally occurring randomness, essentially if these results are naturally distributed (bell curve). It seems that a logical starting point for this check is by comparing the rest of the United States state by state per capita to the population mean (National Per Capita GDP).
